# Preregistration v0.1 — draft, not active

This draft cannot authorize recruitment. It becomes active only after independent ethics/safety review, a frozen protocol hash, a public timestamped registration, and reviewed Spanish/English consent.

## Population and sample

- Consenting adults (18+) using nonclinical online research; demo-only records are excluded unless separately contributed.
- Pilot target: 60 complete participants. Enrollment cap: 90, to allow exclusions without optional stopping.
- No subgroup efficacy claims are confirmatory in this pilot.

## Primary outcome

Within-person difference between paired/omission trials and sham in the predeclared target-response score. A response is scored before opening the dataset from the versioned category and similarity rules in the analysis script.

## Secondary outcomes

- occurrence rate before categories;
- hidden-repeat agreement;
- seven-day retest agreement;
- confidence and expectation dependence;
- completion, audio failures and adverse events.

## Exclusions

Failed audible preflight, incomplete primary block, incompatible protocol/engine hash, duplicated imported session, documented playback failure or self-reported inability to follow the protocol. Every exclusion count and reason is published; excluded adverse events remain in safety reporting.

## Stopping

Enrollment stops at 60 valid completions or 90 enrollments, whichever comes first. It may stop earlier for a serious adverse event plausibly related to the protocol, three moderate events with the same pattern, a material consent/privacy failure, or an unrecoverable protocol defect. It never stops early because a result looks positive or negative.

## Confirmatory contrasts

1. paired/omission target score minus sham;
2. hidden repeat agreement minus nonmatching-pair agreement;
3. delayed retest agreement minus preregistered chance baseline;
4. primary contrast adjusted for trial expectation and order.

## Transparency

Protocol, engine, analysis code, exclusions, deviations, null findings and adverse-event counts are released with the result. Free text and personal data are never made public.
